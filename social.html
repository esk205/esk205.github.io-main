Bystander Effect

Individuals are less likely to help someone in need when others are present, assuming that someone else will intervene.

Example: In emergencies, the more people present, the lower the chances any one person will act.

Cognitive Dissonance

The mental discomfort experienced when holding two contradictory beliefs or values, leading people to change their attitude or belief to reduce discomfort.

Example: Justifying an expensive purchase by telling yourself it was "worth it" even if it wasn’t necessary.

The Halo Effect

A positive impression of someone in one area (like appearance) leads to the assumption that they excel in other areas.

Example: Assuming a good-looking person is also intelligent or kind.

The Pygmalion Effect

High expectations can lead to improved performance, while low expectations can hinder progress.

Example: A teacher’s belief that certain students will excel leads to those students actually doing better.
