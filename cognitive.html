Sunken Cost Fallacy

The tendency to continue investing in a decision, even when it no longer benefits, just because you've already spent time, money, or effort on it.

Example: Staying in a bad movie because you paid for the ticket.

Confirmation Bias

The tendency to search for, interpret, and remember information that confirms existing beliefs, while ignoring evidence that contradicts them.

Example: A person who believes in astrology notices only the times it "predicts" something right.

Anchoring Effect

People rely heavily on the first piece of information (the "anchor") they encounter when making decisions.

Example: A product seems cheaper just because it’s next to something much more expensive.

Placebo Effect

When a person experiences a perceived benefit from a treatment that has no active ingredients, purely due to the belief that it works.

Example: Feeling better after taking a sugar pill, thinking it’s real medicine.
