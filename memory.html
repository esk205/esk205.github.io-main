The Mandela Effect

A phenomenon where large groups of people remember something differently from how it actually occurred.

Example: Many people misremember Nelson Mandela dying in prison when he didn’t.

Change Blindness

A failure to notice significant changes in one’s environment when attention is focused elsewhere.

Example: In experiments, participants fail to notice when a person they’re interacting with is swapped with someone else mid-conversation.

False Memory

The tendency to overestimate how much others notice or care about one’s appearance or actions.

Example: Believing you had a childhood experience because others told you about it, even though it never occurred

The Spotlight Effect

The spotlight effect is a psychological phenomenon where people overestimate how much others notice or care about their actions, appearance, or mistakes. It stems from our tendency to feel like we're at the center of others' attention, even though people generally pay far less attention than we think.

Example: Feeling self-conscious about wearing mismatched socks, thinking everyone will notice.
